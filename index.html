About the Lab

What is the GCI Print Farm?

A hands-on fabrication lab where students learn the complete 3D printing workflow — from finding and designing models to slicing, printing, and quality inspection.

🖨️

Bambu Lab Printers

Three industry-grade printer models: the A1 for large multi-color prints, A1 Mini for quick desktop work, and P1S for high-temperature enclosed prints.

🎓

Structured Training

A five-day guided program takes students from printer basics all the way through designing and printing an original school logo keychain.

🤝

Student Operated

Students manage print jobs, maintain equipment, help peers troubleshoot, and run the lab day-to-day — real-world experience from day one.

Training Program

5-Day Intro to 3D Printing

Each day builds on the last — start with the basics and finish with a complete printed project.

Day 1 Foundations

Foundations of 3D Printing

How printers work, key parts, filament types, and exploring MakerWorld for the first time.
Day 2 Software

From Model to Print Setup

Model selection, introduction to Bambu Studio, print settings, and basic troubleshooting.
Day 3 Design

Designing for 3D Printing

File types (STL, 3MF, G-code), Tinkercad design workflow, and exporting for Bambu Studio.
Day 4 Quality

Troubleshoot and Reflect

Inspect finished prints, diagnose common issues, and document your results and observations.
Day 5 Capstone

Capstone — Remix and Improve

Redesign or remix a model from scratch, present your work, and plan your next print.

What You'll Learn

Learning Outcomes

By the end of the training program, students will be able to:

  • Identify the key parts of a Bambu Lab 3D printer
  • Print from MakerWorld with confidence
  • Use Bambu Studio to slice and prepare print files
  • Understand the full 3D printing workflow — design to object
  • Create and print an original school logo keychain
  • Perform weekly printer maintenance tasks
  • Diagnose and troubleshoot common print failures
