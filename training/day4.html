📚 Day 4 Vocabulary — Logo, SVG & Printable Design

Review these terms before you start. They'll help you understand the steps ahead. 📖 Open Full Glossary

SVG (Scalable Vector Graphics) — A file format that stores images as mathematical shapes rather than pixels. Tinkercad can read SVG files and convert them into 3D shapes.
Vector Image — An image made of lines and curves that can be scaled to any size without becoming blurry. SVG files are vector images.
Raster Image — An image made of pixels (like a photo or PNG). Raster images lose quality when enlarged. You must convert them to SVG before importing into Tinkercad.
Logo Complexity — How many shapes, details, and fine elements a logo has. Simple logos with bold shapes print much better at keychain scale than complex logos with thin lines and small text.
Keyring Hole — The small hole at the top of a keychain that a ring or clip passes through. In Tinkercad, you create this by subtracting a cylinder hole from the keychain body.
3MF — The print-ready file format saved from Bambu Studio. It stores your model plus all the slicer settings in one file. You'll save your keychain as a 3MF.
Silhouette — The outer outline of a shape or logo when viewed as a solid black form. A strong, recognizable silhouette usually means a logo will print well.
Resolution (Print) — How much detail the printer can reproduce. Fine lines thinner than about 1–2 mm may not print clearly, especially at keychain scale.
Step 1 of 8 Active 🔍 Get Your Logo
📌 Goal: Find a clean, simple version of your school logo. Simpler is better — the printer can't handle tiny details.
  • Search Google Images for your school logo or mascot
  • Choose the simplest version you can find — bold shapes print cleanly, tiny details don't
  • Download it as PNG or JPG

🆚 Simple vs. Complicated Logos

Not all logos print equally well. Before you pick one, understand the difference:

✅ Simple Logo — Good for Printing
⭐
🟢 Bold, thick shapes 🟢 Minimal fine lines 🟢 Clear silhouette 🟢 No tiny text 🟢 Easy to convert to SVG

A simple star, letter, or bold mascot shape will print cleanly, hold up well as a keychain, and be easy to convert to SVG in Adobe Express.

❌ Complicated Logo — Harder to Print
🔱
🔴 Tiny thin lines 🔴 Small text details 🔴 Complex gradients 🔴 Overlapping shapes 🔴 Hard to clean up in SVG

A detailed crest with thin lines, small text, and overlapping shapes will be hard to convert cleanly, difficult to scale in Tinkercad, and may lose key details when printed at keychain size.

💡 How to Choose a Logo That Will Print Successfully:
  • Bold shapes win — thick outlines and filled areas print clearly at keychain size (40–60 mm)
  • Avoid tiny text — letters smaller than about 5 mm will blur or disappear in the print
  • Fewer shapes = easier SVG — the more separate pieces a logo has, the harder it is to clean up
  • Check the silhouette — could you recognize your logo if it was a solid black shape? If yes, it'll probably print well
  • When in doubt, simplify — it's always better to print a clean simple design than a messy complex one
💡 When in doubt, go simpler. Fine lines and tiny text rarely survive the print.
Step 2 of 8 Locked 🔄 Convert to SVG
📌 Goal: Turn your logo into an SVG so Tinkercad can read it as a 3D shape.

First — Sign In to Adobe Express

  • Go to Adobe Express
  • Sign in with your school Google account

🎥 Watch and Follow

  • Upload your logo
  • Remove the background if there is one
  • Download it as SVG
✅ Checkpoint: Your file should end in .svg
Step 3 of 8 Locked 📦 Import into Tinkercad
📌 Goal: Import your SVG into Tinkercad and scale it to keychain size.

🎥 Watch and Follow

  • Open Tinkercad, sign in, and start a new design
  • Click Import and upload your SVG
  • Scale it to roughly 40–60 mm wide
Step 4 of 8 Locked 🔑 Make It a Keychain
📌 Goal: Give your logo shape depth and add a keyring hole — that's what makes it a keychain.
  • Set the thickness to 3–5 mm — thin enough to be light, thick enough to be durable
  • Add a cylinder hole near one edge for the keyring (about 4–5 mm diameter)
  • Group everything together before you export
💡 Quick Tips:
  • Tiny details get lost in the print — keep it bold
  • Thick, solid shapes give the cleanest results
Step 5 of 8 Locked ⚙️ Export + Slice
📌 Goal: Get your design out of Tinkercad and into Bambu Studio, ready to slice.

Export from Tinkercad

  • Click Export → STL

In Bambu Studio

  • Import your STL
  • Lay it flat on the build plate
  • Check that filament is set to the right color
  • Click Slice
  • Preview the layers to make sure it looks right
Step 6 of 8 Locked 💾 Save Your File
📌 Goal: Save your 3MF with the right name so the print queue knows it's yours.
  • In Bambu Studio, save your project as a 3MF file
  • File name: LastName_Keychain
✅ Your file should end in .3mf and be named exactly as shown.
