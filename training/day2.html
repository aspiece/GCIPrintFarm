Step 1 of 7 Active Day 2 Share-Out
📌 Instructions: Get into groups of 4–5. Each person shares the model they found on MakerWorld yesterday. You have about 2 minutes each.

Discussion Prompts

  • What model did you find? What does it do or look like?
  • Why did it stand out to you?
  • Would it be realistic to print in our lab?
  • What surprised you about MakerWorld?
💬 Heads up: There are no wrong answers here — sharing your curiosity is the whole point!
Step 2 of 7 Locked Choose a Good Print
📌 Instructions: Open MakerWorld and browse for a model you would realistically want to print in this class. Think about size, complexity, and purpose.

What Makes a Good Choice?

  • Fits on our printer (max ~256 × 256 × 256 mm)
  • Designed for FDM (not resin-only)
  • Not too complex for a first or second print
  • Something you actually want to make

❌ Weak Answer

"It looks cool and I want it."

This doesn't show understanding of what makes a print realistic or challenging.

✅ Strong Answer

"This phone stand is about 120 mm wide, so it fits easily. It has a few small overhangs but Bambu Studio can auto-add supports. The purpose is clear and it's something I'd actually use."

Specific, realistic, and shows print-prep thinking.
