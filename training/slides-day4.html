Simple vs. Complex — Which Prints Better?

✅ Simple Logo — Great for Printing
  • 🟢 Bold, thick shapes with clear outlines
  • 🟢 Minimal fine lines or detail
  • 🟢 Strong, recognizable silhouette
  • 🟢 No tiny text or microscopic details
  • 🟢 Converts cleanly — generator processes it easily
  • 🟢 Easy to scale to keychain size
❌ Complex Logo — Printing Challenges
  • 🔴 Many thin lines and micro-details
  • 🔴 Small text within the logo
  • 🔴 Overlapping shapes and gradients
  • 🔴 Multiple colors (we print in one color)
  • 🔴 SVG conversion creates messy paths
  • 🔴 Generator may not process cleanly
6 / 21
  • Use real examples if you have them. Pull up 2 logos on your browser — one simple school logo and one complex crest. Ask "Which would you choose?"
  • Key teaching moment: "3D printing is a single color, single material process for us. A logo that depends on color to be readable (like the colors of a flag) won't translate to a 3D print — the shape is all you have."
  • Good rule of thumb to share: "Imagine your logo as a shadow — just a flat black silhouette. If you can still tell what it is, it will probably print well. If it's unrecognizable as a silhouette, look for a simpler version."
  • Student action: After this slide, give students 60 seconds to look at their logo and decide if it's simple or complex. Discuss as needed.
⏱ 3 min

Why Simple Designs Print Better

  • Printer nozzle is 0.4 mm wide — it cannot reproduce lines thinner than that
  • At keychain scale (50 mm), tiny details disappear or merge together
  • Complex logos with many separate shapes may not process cleanly in the Keychain Generator
  • Simple outlines = stronger walls and cleaner prints
🎯

A logo that prints clearly at 50 mm is more impressive than a complex logo that comes out as a blob.

Clean beats complicated.

7 / 21
  • The 0.4 mm nozzle fact is powerful — students immediately understand why hair-thin lines won't print.
  • Relate to scale: A 1 cm wide element on a screen becomes 5 mm when scaled for a keychain. If it was already thin on screen, at 5 mm it disappears entirely.
  • Encourage simplification: "If your logo has a complicated crest, see if there's a simpler version — often there's a logo-mark or icon version that's just the mascot head or a letter. That's the one to use."
⏱ 3 min

Choosing a Printable Logo

💡 5 Rules for a Logo That Will Print Successfully:

  • Bold shapes win — thick outlines and filled areas print clearly at keychain size (40–60 mm)
  • Avoid tiny text — letters smaller than ~5 mm will blur or disappear in the print
  • Fewer shapes = cleaner result — the more separate pieces a logo has, the harder it is for the generator to process it cleanly
  • Check the silhouette — could you recognize your logo if it was a solid black shape? If yes, it'll print well
  • When in doubt, simplify — a clean simple design always beats a messy complex one at keychain scale

🎯 Step 1

Get Your Logo Image

  • Go to Google Images and search: "[your school] logo PNG transparent"
  • Look for the simplest version you can find — skip the detailed crest if there's a simpler option
  • Right-click → Save Image As → save to your Desktop
  • Preferred format: PNG with transparent background (easier to convert)
  • If only JPG is available — that will work too
💡 If you already have your logo from yesterday — great! Skip the search and go straight to the Keychain Generator.
9 / 21
  • Monitor during this 4 minutes. Students sometimes find overly complex logos or download a logo that's not for their school. Redirect quickly.
  • PNG with transparent background is best — a logo on a white background may cause the generator to include the white background as part of the 3D shape. Transparent or solid-color background on a contrasting logo works best.
  • If a student can't find a good logo: Have a backup collection ready — a folder of clean PNG logos for local schools or mascot silhouettes. This prevents a 10-minute search derailing the whole session.

🎓 Step 2

Upload to Keychain Generator

How to upload

  • Go to teachingtech.site/3D/keychain
  • Click Choose File (or drag your logo onto the upload area)
  • Select your PNG or JPG logo
  • Wait a moment — the generator processes the image and shows a 3D preview
  • Rotate and zoom the preview to check the logo shape looks right

What a good preview looks like

  • Logo shape is clearly recognizable in the 3D preview
  • Clean raised relief — not a blob or a mess of thin lines
  • Background is not part of the 3D shape
⚠️ If the preview looks wrong, try a simpler logo image — good contrast between logo and background helps the generator.
10 / 21
  • Demo on projector first — upload a sample logo and show the 3D preview live. Let students see what a clean result looks like before they do it themselves.
  • Common issues: (1) White background included as a flat slab — student needs a logo with transparent or contrasting background. (2) Very complex logo creates a messy preview — redirect to a simpler version. (3) Generator slow to respond — normal; give it 10–15 seconds.
  • Quality check: Walk around and look at each student's 3D preview. If the shape is unrecognizable, they need a simpler logo. Intervene early.
  • Transition: "Brain break next — when you come back, we're configuring the settings and adding the hole."

🎓 Step 3

Import SVG into TinkerCAD

How to import

  • In the Keychain Generator, find the Settings panel (usually on the right or below the preview)
  • Set Width to 40–60 mm — fits well in hand and on the build plate
  • Set Depth / Thickness to 3–5 mm — strong enough to use, light enough to carry
  • Make sure Base layer is enabled — gives the logo something to sit on
  • Check the preview after each change — the model should update live

Good settings quick reference

  • Width: 40–60 mm
  • Thickness: 3–5 mm
  • Base layer: On
💡 Wider isn't always better — at keychain scale bold shapes show up cleanly, tiny lines don't.
13 / 21
  • Live demo immediately: Show the settings panel on the projector. Change width, thickness, toggle base layer and watch the preview update. Do it slowly so students can follow along.
  • Common mistake: Students set the width too large (100 mm+) — the keychain won't fit on the build plate or will take too long to print. Keep it under 60 mm.
  • Base layer: This creates a solid backing behind the logo relief — important for strength. If a student turns it off, the logo may be too thin to survive as a keychain.
⏱ 8 min
🎯 Step 4 — Required

Add the Keyring Hole — Required

⚠️ A keychain without a hole is just a charm. This step is required.

  • In the generator settings, find the Hole or Keyring option and enable it
  • Set hole diameter to 4–6 mm — fits a standard keyring
  • Make sure there is at least 2 mm of solid material around the hole — if the walls are too thin, it will snap
  • Position: near the top edge of the keychain (most generators place it automatically)
  • Verify the hole is visible in the 3D preview — it should appear as a circular cutout
✋ Hole check before exporting: 4–6 mm diameter, ≥2 mm material around it. Without it, your design won't be approved to print.
14 / 21
  • Walk the room during this step. The hole is a blocking requirement — no hole = no print approval. Check that every student has it enabled and the preview shows the cutout.
  • Common issues: (1) Hole enabled but diameter too small (<3 mm) — standard keyring won't fit. (2) Hole placed at the bottom instead of the top — cosmetically fine but functionally awkward. (3) Student can't find the hole toggle — help them locate it in the generator UI.
  • For advanced students: Suggest adjusting the hole position or adding their name/initials if the generator supports it.
  • After everyone has a valid hole: Pause the class and have 2 students show their preview on the projector.
⏱ 3 min
📚 Vocabulary

Key Terms — Day 4

Keychain Generator A free web tool that turns an uploaded image into a printable 3D keychain. It handles the base shape, thickness, and keyring hole automatically.
Keyring Hole The circular cutout at the top of a keychain that a ring passes through. Must be 4–6 mm wide with at least 2 mm of material around it for strength.
Silhouette The outer shape of a design when viewed as a solid. A strong silhouette = a design that prints clearly at small scale.
Orientation How the model sits on the build plate. Lay the keychain flat (face down) for the strongest result and cleanest top surface.
15 / 21
  • Quick vocabulary pause during work time — review while students continue working.
  • Ask: "Why does orientation matter for a keychain?" (Face-down = the logo surface is printed on the plate = smooth finish on the logo side. On its side = logo surface is a vertical wall = rougher quality.)
  • Transition: "Last two steps — export and slice."
⏱ 5 min
🎓 Step 5

Export STL + Slice in Bambu Studio

Export from the Keychain Generator

  • Click Download STL (or Export)
  • Save to your Desktop or Downloads folder

In Bambu Studio

  • Drag the STL onto the build plate or use File → Import
  • Make sure keychain is lying flat (face down) — right-click → Lay on Face if needed
  • Settings: 0.2 mm layer height · 20% infill · supports off
  • Click Slice Plate → review Preview
  • Check print time (< 2 hours is fine)

Save the 3MF file

  • After slicing → File → Save Project
  • Filename: LastName_Keychain.3mf
  • This is the file you'll send to the printer on Day 5
  • Keep both STL (from the generator) and 3MF (print-ready)
16 / 21
  • This is the most critical file management moment of the entire lab. A lost 3MF on print day = student can't print. Be explicit about where files should be saved.
  • Naming convention is not optional: "If your file is named 'keychain.3mf' and so is your neighbor's, we have a problem on print day. Your name must be in the filename."
  • What to check in Preview: (1) Keychain is lying flat (face down). (2) Keyring hole goes all the way through — visible in the layer preview. (3) First layer is solid. (4) Print time is reasonable.
  • If the model is on its side in Bambu Studio: Right-click → Lay on Face to flip it flat. Printing on its side weakens the keychain.
⏱ 3 min
✅ Print Readiness Checklist

Is Your Design Ready to Print?

  • Size is correct (40–60 mm in the longest dimension)
  • Thick enough (3–5 mm) — nothing thinner than 2 mm around the hole
  • Keyring hole present (4–6 mm, visible as a cutout through the preview)
  • Model is lying flat (face down) on the Bambu Studio build plate
  • File sliced correctly — Preview looks right
  • Saved as 3MF with correct name: LastName_Keychain.3mf
17 / 21
  • Walk through this list with students as a whole class, then give 2 minutes for students to verify each item on their own machine.
  • Teacher sign-off: Consider having each student show you the Bambu Studio Preview before you sign off. A 30-second check per student during this time is realistic.
  • Students who aren't print-ready: Common reasons: logo not recognizable in the preview, hole missing or too small, model on its side, or file naming issues. Address before exit ticket.
