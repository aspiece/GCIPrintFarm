⏱ 5 min

File Types: STL · 3MF · G-code

📄

STL

Standard format. Stores only the 3D shape as a mesh of triangles. No color, no settings, no print info.

Created in: Tinkercad
Opened by: Bambu Studio

📦

3MF

Newer format. Stores shape + materials + color + print settings in one file. The preferred format for Bambu.

Created by: Bambu Studio (sliced)
Sent to: Printer

🖨️

G-code

Machine language. Exact movement + temperature instructions for every second of the print. Created by slicing.

Created by: Slicer
Read by: Printer firmware

5 / 20
  • The workflow: TinkerCAD → STL → Bambu Studio (slice) → 3MF/G-code → printer. Each step creates a different file type.
  • Practical tip for students: "Always save your TinkerCAD design to your account in TinkerCAD. Then export STL for slicing. Then save the sliced 3MF from Bambu Studio. You need all three — don't delete any of them."
  • Ask: "Which of these three files does the printer actually read?" (G-code / 3MF — both contain G-code.)
⏱ 3 min
📚 Vocabulary

Key Terms — TinkerCAD & Design

Workplane The flat surface in TinkerCAD where you place shapes. All objects are built relative to this surface.
Hole A TinkerCAD shape set to "Hole" mode. When grouped with a solid, it cuts that shape out — perfect for the keyring hole.
Group Combining shapes together. Grouping a hole shape with a solid shape cuts the void — finalizes the keyring hole cutout.
Export Saving your design as STL or 3MF. Required to move your model from TinkerCAD into Bambu Studio.
6 / 20
  • The Hole concept is counterintuitive — spend an extra 30 seconds on it. "You're not creating nothing — you're creating a 'negative' shape. When you group it with something solid, it removes that volume."
  • Visual tip: Show a cylinder, turn it to Hole, then group it with a box — students immediately understand when they see it happen in real time.
  • Transition: "Let's watch TinkerCAD in action. Pay attention to how shapes and holes work together."

What is TinkerCAD?

  • Free, browser-based 3D design tool made by Autodesk
  • No download required — works in Chrome, Firefox, Safari
  • Designed for beginners — uses simple shapes as building blocks
  • You add shapes or subtract them (using holes)
  • Used by students, hobbyists, engineers, and professionals
🏗️

TinkerCAD is like digital LEGOs. You snap shapes together (and punch holes out of them) to build anything you can imagine.

🎓 Interface Tour

TinkerCAD — Key Areas

🖱️ Viewport

  • Center of the screen
  • Right-click + drag = rotate view
  • Scroll = zoom in/out
  • Middle-click + drag = pan

📐 Top Toolbar

  • Group button (Ctrl+G)
  • Ungroup button (Ctrl+Shift+G)
  • Hole / Solid toggle
  • Align and Mirror tools

➡️ Right Panel

  • Shape library — drag onto workplane
  • Basic Shapes: Box, Cylinder, Sphere
  • Scroll down for more categories
11 / 20
  • Show each area live on TinkerCAD as you describe it. Students should have their machines open — follow along.
  • Most important shortcuts to teach: Ctrl+G (Group), Ctrl+Z (Undo), Ctrl+D (Duplicate). Write these on the board.
  • Common early frustration: Students accidentally rotate the view when they want to move a shape. Clarify: click the shape first to select it, THEN drag it. Don't drag without selecting.
  • Home view tip: If students get lost in the viewport (disoriented), press F to fit everything in view, or use the cube in the top-right corner to reset orientation.
⏱ 4 min
🎓 Key Tools

TinkerCAD — Essential Operations

Building

  • Drag a shape from the right panel onto the workplane
  • Click white handles to resize
  • Click the shape and drag to move it
  • Right-click → Duplicate (or Ctrl+D)

Holes & Grouping

  • Select a shape → click Hole in top toolbar
  • Position the hole shape over your solid shape
  • Select BOTH shapes → click Group (Ctrl+G)
  • The hole is now cut out — permanent until ungrouped
⚠️ You MUST Group a hole with its solid shape — otherwise the hole has no effect!
12 / 20
  • Live demo on projector: Create a box, drag a cylinder onto it, set cylinder to Hole, Group them. The whole room should see the cylinder disappear from the box. This "aha" moment is worth 2 minutes of demo time.
  • Critical mistake to prevent: Students set a shape to Hole but forget to Group. Then nothing gets cut. Circulate and check: if you see a transparent cylinder just sitting on a box, they forgot to Group.
  • Undo is your friend: Ctrl+Z undoes in TinkerCAD. Encourage students to experiment boldly — every mistake is undoable.

⏱ 3 min

Design Tips for Keychains

✅ Do this

  • Keep the keychain 40–60 mm in length (actual keychain size)
  • Make walls and text at least 2 mm thick — thin features break off
  • Place the keyring hole at least 3 mm from any edge
  • Keep the design flat (low profile) for better bed adhesion

⚠️ Avoid this

  • Extremely thin spires or walls (< 1 mm) — they snap
  • Overhangs that need supports (adds complexity)
  • Designs much taller than 5 mm (wobble risk)
  • Tiny text below ~4pt equivalent — letters won't print clearly
14 / 20
  • Print an example keychain (if you have one) and pass it around while reviewing these tips. Physical reference beats slides every time.
  • Keyring hole size tip: The hole should be at least 5 mm in diameter to fit a standard keyring. Show students how to check the hole size in TinkerCAD (dimensions appear when you click a shape).
  • Thickness matters most: More failed keychains result from thin walls than any other issue. When in doubt, make it thicker.

🎯 Student Work

Build Your Keychain

In TinkerCAD — start building:

  • Create a base shape for your keychain (rectangle or custom shape)
  • Add any design elements or logos (we'll import SVG tomorrow)
  • Add the keyring hole using a cylinder set to Hole, then Group
  • Scale to approximately 50 × 30 mm
  • Save your design in TinkerCAD (auto-saves, but name it clearly)
✋ Don't export yet — make sure your design looks right first. Export is the next step.
15 / 20
  • Circulate constantly during this 15 minutes. The most common issues: (1) Hole not grouped — transparent cylinder just sitting there. (2) Shape too large (100+ mm instead of 50mm). (3) Keyring hole too small (less than 5 mm).
  • Encourage experimentation: "Try putting a text shape on your keychain. Text in TinkerCAD is under the Shape panel → Text."
  • For students who finish quickly: Ask them to help a neighbor, OR have them add additional design details, OR start thinking about their school logo placement for Day 4.
  • Before advancing: Pause the class and ask 2–3 students to share their design on the projector. This sparks ideas and normalizes different approaches.
⏱ 4 min
🎓 Walkthrough

Export Your Design

How to export from TinkerCAD

  • Click Export button (top right)
  • Choose .STL format
  • File downloads to your Downloads folder
  • Rename it: YourName_Keychain_v1.stl

Then open in Bambu Studio

  • Drag the STL file into Bambu Studio
  • Verify the model looks correct
  • Scale if needed (should be ~50 mm)
  • You don't need to slice yet — just check it looks right
📌 Naming convention matters — FirstName_Keychain_v1.stl prevents lost files on Day 5
16 / 20
  • Demo the export live — Export from TinkerCAD on your machine, then drag into Bambu Studio and show the result on the projector.
  • Naming convention: This is not optional. On print day you'll have 20+ files on your machine. Students who use vague names ("keychain.stl") cause confusion. Enforce the naming standard today.
  • Common export issue: Student exports the whole project instead of the selected shape. Make sure they select all shapes first (Ctrl+A) or select just what they want before exporting.
  • Bambu Studio check: If the model appears correct in Bambu Studio (right size, right shape, no obvious gaps), the design is good. Students don't need to slice today — that's Day 5.

✅ Check for Understanding — 5 Questions

Quick Check

  1. Students under 12 should click which button to create a TinkerCAD account?
  2. Where are shapes located in TinkerCAD? (Be specific.)
  3. How do you delete a shape in TinkerCAD? (Two methods.)
  4. What does setting a shape to Hole do when you Group it with a solid?
  5. What is the correct order? Export → Design → Group or Design → Group → Export?
17 / 20
  • Answers: (1) "Join a class" button. (2) Right side panel. (3) Click shape + Delete key, OR trash can icon. (4) It cuts that shape's volume out of the solid when grouped. (5) Design → Group → Export.
  • Q4 is the most important. If students can't answer it, the Hole tool demo needs to be repeated — have them try it one more time on their own machine before moving on.
  • Use cold-calling strategically — pick students who seemed confused during the work time for Q3 and Q4.
  • Transition: "Great. Last step — submit your work for today."
